Threat Hunting Summary

Threat hunting is a proactive security activity where analysts search through logs and system data to identify potential threats that may not have triggered alerts.

During this exercise, logs were analyzed to identify suspicious authentication behavior related to MITRE ATT&CK technique T1078 (Valid Accounts). This technique involves attackers using legitimate credentials to gain unauthorized access to systems.

By reviewing authentication logs, analysts can detect abnormal login patterns such as logins from unusual locations, multiple failed login attempts followed by a successful login, or logins occurring outside normal working hours.

Threat intelligence and behavioral analysis together help SOC analysts identify hidden threats before they escalate into serious security incidents.

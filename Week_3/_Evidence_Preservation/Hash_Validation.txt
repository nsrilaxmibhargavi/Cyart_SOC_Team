Hash Validation Summary

To ensure the integrity of collected evidence, cryptographic hashing was performed on the acquired data. The SHA-256 hashing algorithm was used to generate a unique hash value for each evidence file.

Hash validation ensures that the collected evidence has not been altered during storage or analysis. If the hash value changes, it indicates that the file integrity has been compromised.

During the investigation process, the memory dump collected from Server-Y was hashed and recorded in the evidence documentation table. This allows investigators to verify the authenticity of the evidence throughout the incident response process.

Maintaining evidence integrity is a critical step in digital forensics and incident response.
